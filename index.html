Early life

Born: October 28, 1955, in Seattle, Washington, USA. Family: His father was a lawyer, and his mother was a teacher and business leader. Early Interest in Computing: At age 13, he started programming on a Teletype terminal at his school. He created his first software program—a tic-tac-toe game—on a General Electric (GE) computer. He later hacked into his school’s computer system to gain more programming time.

Harvard and the Birth of Microsoft

Harvard University (1973): Studied mathematics and computer science but dropped out in 1975. Founded Microsoft (1975): Along with Paul Allen, he started Microsoft in Albuquerque, New Mexico. First Big Break (1980): Microsoft licensed MS-DOS to IBM for its first personal computer.

Microsoft’s Rise and Windows Revolution (1980s–1990s)

1985: Microsoft released Windows 1.0, an early graphical user interface. 1990: Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint) launched, changing business productivity forever. 1995: Windows 95 became a global success, making personal computers more user-friendly. By the late 1990s, Microsoft was the dominant software company, and Gates was the richest person in the world.

Founder of Microsoft

The original purpose of Microsoft was to develop and sell BASIC interpreters for the Altair 8800, an early personal computer developed by MITS (Micro Instrumentation and Telemetry Systems). BASIC (Beginner’s All-purpose Symbolic Instruction Code) was a popular programming language at the time, and Microsoft’s version allowed users to write and execute programs on the Altair 8800 efficiently.

Windows

The first version of Microsoft Windows, Windows 1.0, was released on November 20, 1985. Its primary purpose was to provide a graphical user interface (GUI) that made personal computing more user-friendly and accessible, especially for people who were used to command-line interfaces.

Microsoft Office

Microsoft Office was first released on August 1, 1989, for Macintosh computers and later for Windows in 1990. Microsoft Office was created to provide a suite of productivity applications that help users create, edit, and manage documents, spreadsheets, presentations, and emails.

Internet Explorer

Internet Explorer (IE) was created by Microsoft and first released on August 16, 1995. Its purpose was web browsing, integration with Windows, competition with Netscape, and support for web technologies.
